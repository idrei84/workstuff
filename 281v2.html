EAP 0281 – Draft Integrated Skills Learning Outcomes

Section 1
Supporting Details in High-Beginning-Level Texts (Spoken and Written)
Comprehend
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Identify active reading/thinking strategies (previewing, predicting, scanning, and skimming)
  • Identify the topic of a reading passage
  • Identify basic information in academic texts (who, what, where, when, why, how)
  • Identify/recognize supporting details in a reading passage and conversation
Apply
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Utilize active reading/thinking strategies (previewing, predicting, scanning, and skimming) to identify supporting details
  • Utilize active reading/thinking strategies (previewing, predicting, scanning, and skimming) to identify main ideas
  • Demonstrate understanding of supporting details from a variety of texts (a reading passage, a lecture, a conversation) by asking and responding to questions
  • Utilize supporting details and topics to identify main ideas
Produce
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Communicate and write supporting details related to the main idea
  • Summarize the main idea of a paragraph in 1–2 sentences using appropriate formality
  • Explain ideas orally using formal or informal language depending on context
  • Use main ideas and supporting details to answer comprehension questions in the correct format
Section 2
Academic Vocabulary in High-Beginning-Level Texts (Spoken and Written)
Comprehend
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Identify and understand key academic vocabulary related to the lesson topic
  • Identify and recognize academic vocabulary in short texts, lectures, explanations, and conversations
  • Identify types of context clues (examples, pictures, graphs, and general sense)
Apply
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Use context clues to determine the meaning of new academic vocabulary
  • Demonstrate understanding of academic vocabulary in reading, writing, listening, and discussions
  • Match words to definitions, pictures, graphs, or examples in texts and conversations
  • Apply academic vocabulary to identify main ideas and supporting details
  • Apply academic vocabulary to express main ideas and supporting details
  • Use academic vocabulary related to the lesson topic
  • Use academic vocabulary in lectures, explanations, and conversations
  • Use context clues (examples, pictures, graphs, and general sense)
  • Use selected academic vocabulary words when discussing the topic with a partner, in class, and in writing
Produce
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Use selected academic vocabulary words when discussing the topic with a partner, in class, and in writing
  • Use the correct spelling of academic vocabulary words
  • Use the correct pronunciation of academic vocabulary words
  • Include academic vocabulary in communication (written sentences, short paragraphs, speeches, presentations)
Section 3
Informal and Formal Modes of Communication in High-Beginning-Level Texts (Short Answer/Paragraph, Speech, Presentation)
Comprehend
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Distinguish between informal and formal modes of communication in spoken and written texts
  • Comprehend grammar structures in written and oral communication appropriate to the context
  • Identify appropriate word choice and level of formality in short texts or conversations
  • Recognize basic punctuation, capitalization, and sentence structures consistent with the communication context
Apply
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Apply simple and compound sentences using "and," "but," and "so" appropriately in context
  • Apply subject-verb agreement for simple subjects, compound subjects, there is/there are, BE verbs, and simple present 3rd person
  • Use word choice and word form consistent with informal or formal communication
  • Apply capitalization rules (first word in a sentence, pronoun I, days, months, proper nouns)
  • Apply punctuation rules (end-of-sentence punctuation and commas in compound sentences)
  • Utilize the writing process (pre-writing, developing, revising, editing) when composing texts
  • Organize ideas based on a topic or visual prompt
Produce
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Deliver a one- to two-minute structured oral response
  • Produce short sentences, messages, or paragraphs using appropriate informal or formal language
  • Use BE verbs, present progressive, simple present, simple past, and simple future verb tenses
  • Use high-frequency irregular verbs
  • Use affirmative and negative statements in the above verb tenses
  • Use yes/no question formation and information questions (WH and how) in the above verb tenses
  • Use present/future modal auxiliaries (ability and permission)
  • Use basic parts of speech
  • Use singular vs. plural nouns
  • Use basic article rules (a, an, the before singular countable nouns)
  • Use subject and object pronouns
  • Use possessive adjectives and nouns
