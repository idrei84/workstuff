Version 1
EAP 0281 – Draft Integrated Skills Learning Outcomes
Draft for faculty review
Section 1
Reading & Listening Comprehension
Students will demonstrate understanding of high-beginning academic reading and listening materials.
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Utilize previewing, predicting, scanning, and skimming (active reading-listening-thinking strategies)
  • Identify the general topic
  • Identify basic information (who, what, where, when, why, how)
  • Utilize supporting details and topic to identify main ideas
  • Demonstrate understanding by asking and responding to questions
Section 2
Vocabulary Use Across Skills
Students will apply academic vocabulary (meanings and word forms) in reading, listening, speaking, and writing.
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Use context clues (examples, visuals, graphs, general sense) to determine meaning
  • Use academic vocabulary from course materials
  • Identify word forms (noun, verb, adjective, adverb forms of a word)
  • Use vocabulary accurately in speaking and writing tasks (word meaning and form)
Section 3
Sentence Construction & Grammar in Context
Students will construct sentences using high-beginning grammar structures.
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Construct simple sentences and compound sentences (and, but, so)
  • Apply basic rules of capitalization (first word in a sentence, pronoun "I," days of the week, months of the year, proper nouns vs. common nouns)
  • Apply basic rules of punctuation (end-of-sentence punctuation, comma in compound sentence)
  • Apply subject-verb agreement (simple subjects, compound subjects with "and," there is/are, BE verbs, simple present 3rd person)
  • Use verb tenses (BE verbs, present progressive, simple present, simple past, simple future) in affirmative, negative, and question structures (yes/no, WH questions)
  • Use high-frequency irregular verbs
  • Use modal auxiliaries (ability and permission)
  • Use subject and object pronouns
  • Use possessive adjectives and nouns
  • Use singular/plural regular nouns and high-frequency irregular nouns
  • Apply basic article rules (a/an/the before singular countable nouns)
Section 4
Writing Development & Process
Students will produce cohesive written responses about a topic.
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Develop writing using pre-writing, drafting, revising, and editing
  • Organize ideas based on a topic or visual prompt
  • Write approximately 10 sentences about the topic or visual prompt
  • Write simple sentences and compound sentences (and, but, so) in connected text
  • Use appropriate word choice and word form consistent with the context
  • Apply capitalization and punctuation rules consistent with EAP 0281
  • Edit sentences to contain a complete subject and verb (and object when needed)
  • Edit for grammatical accuracy consistent with EAP 0281
  • Edit for accurate word choice and word form
  • Distinguish between informal and academic communication
Section 5
Speaking
Students will develop high-beginning speaking skills necessary for participating in classroom discussions and interpersonal communication.
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Distinguish between informal and academic communication
  • Participate in discussions, ask and answer questions, and request clarification
  • Deliver a one- to two-minute structured oral response
  • Produce American English vowel and consonant sounds, word stress, and basic intonation patterns (-s/es and -ed ending sounds, rising and falling intonation in yes/no questions, WH-questions, and statements)
  • Use level-appropriate grammar and vocabulary for clear communication
Section 6
Technology Skills
Demonstrate technology skills needed for course success.
Corresponding Evidence of Learning
  • Utilize the college-approved learning management system
  • Utilize online learning resources related to the coursework
